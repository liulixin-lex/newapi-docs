# 7. Complaints, Appeals, and Contact

## 1. Applicable Scenarios

You may contact GGUUAI through [support@gguuai.com](mailto:support@gguuai.com) for the following matters:

1. Account, sign-in, API key, or security issues;
2. Top-up, deduction, refund, order, or accounting issues;
3. Service failures, unavailable models, configuration errors, or technical support;
4. Privacy requests, data deletion, account cancellation, or personal information processing issues;
5. Appeals regarding violation handling, account freezing, service suspension, or refund refusal;
6. Reports about intellectual property, infringement, illegal content, AUP violations, or security vulnerabilities;
7. Consultations about service regions, compliance, upstream models, or high-risk uses.

## 2. Appeal Materials

To help us handle appeals, refunds, privacy requests, or complaints, please provide as much of the following as possible:

1. Account email or user ID;
2. Order number, request ID, last four characters of the API key, or relevant time;
3. Problem description and expected handling method;
4. Relevant screenshots, logs, payment proof, business-purpose explanation, or compliance proof;
5. An email address or other contact method where we can reach you.

GGUUAI may require additional materials based on security, risk-control, payment, privacy, or compliance needs. If you refuse to provide necessary materials, or if the materials cannot prove that your request is reasonable, GGUUAI may be unable to handle the request or may reject it.

## 3. Handling Principles

GGUUAI will process requests in a reasonable order. General inquiries, technical support, and ordinary refund requests are usually handled within 3 business days. Matters involving security, risk control, payment chargebacks, upstream providers, privacy verification, regulatory requirements, judicial procedures, or complex disputes may take longer.

For serious violations, child safety, malware, attacks, fraud, stolen-card use, money laundering, cash-out activity, payment risks, sanctions or export-control risks, regulatory risks, or mandatory upstream requirements, GGUUAI may take necessary restrictive measures first and then conduct follow-up review.
