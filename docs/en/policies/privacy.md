# 4. Privacy Policy and Data Processing Notice

## 1. Basic Principles

GGUUAI respects and protects user privacy and data security. We process personal information and related data according to the principles of legality, legitimacy, necessity, good faith, clear purpose, data minimization, and security protection.

This Privacy Policy explains the information GGUUAI may collect, use, retain, share, transfer, delete, and protect when providing API relay, model aggregation, console, billing, risk control, customer support, and related services.

## 2. Information We May Collect

To provide and maintain the service, GGUUAI may collect the following information:

1. Account information: username, email address, sign-in method, authentication information, organization information, team information, role permissions, and contact information;
2. Payment and order information: order number, top-up amount, payment status, payment channel, refund records, invoice or accounting information, and necessary payment or receiving proof;
3. API call metadata: request time, model name, usage, consumption, response status, error code, request ID, routing information, latency, concurrency, rate limits, and cache status;
4. Security and risk-control information: IP address, device information, browser or client information, sign-in records, abnormal call records, key usage records, and risk-control review records;
5. Customer support and communication information: questions, feedback, appeals, tickets, emails, attachments, and handling records you submit to us;
6. User content: inputs, files, context, parameters, outputs, or related data submitted through the API by you or your end users, but only within the scope necessary to provide the service, route requests, bill usage, troubleshoot issues, maintain security, comply with obligations, handle user submissions, support user-enabled logging, or meet legal and regulatory requirements.

## 3. How We Use Information

GGUUAI may use relevant information for the following purposes:

1. Create, maintain, and manage accounts;
2. Provide API relay, model aggregation, model routing, console, and related functions;
3. Perform identity verification, permission control, API key management, and security protection;
4. Calculate usage, deduct fees, process top-ups, refunds, account reconciliation, and order management;
5. Troubleshoot failures, optimize services, analyze errors, and improve stability;
6. Identify, prevent, and handle abuse, attacks, fraud, stolen-card use, chargebacks, money laundering, cash-out activity, abnormal refunds, restriction evasion, and other risks;
7. Enforce the Acceptable Use Policy, Terms of Service, upstream requirements, and applicable laws and regulations;
8. Respond to customer support, complaints, appeals, privacy requests, and security incidents;
9. Send service notices, terms updates, price changes, maintenance notices, and security reminders;
10. Fulfill compliance requirements from laws and regulations, regulators, judicial authorities, payment channels, or upstream providers.

## 4. API Content and Model Training

By default, GGUUAI does not use inputs or outputs submitted by users through the API to train general-purpose models.

If GGUUAI later offers development cooperation, data authorization, product improvement, model evaluation, feedback data use, fine-tuning, model customization, or similar programs, GGUUAI will use authorized data under the corresponding terms only after you explicitly opt in, authorize, or sign the relevant agreement.

You understand and agree that, as an API relay and model aggregation service, GGUUAI may need to send your requests, inputs, parameters, files, or context to the upstream model provider you choose or the platform routes to in order to complete model calls. Different upstream providers may have different data retention, review, training, logging, security, and privacy policies. Upstream processing of data is governed by their applicable terms, policies, model rules, and your configuration choices.

Except as necessary to provide the service, route requests, bill usage, troubleshoot issues, maintain security, perform risk control, comply with obligations, meet legal requirements, or support user configuration, GGUUAI will not actively use your API inputs and outputs to train general-purpose models.

## 5. Data Retention Periods

GGUUAI retains relevant information for the shortest period necessary to achieve the processing purpose, except where laws and regulations, regulatory requirements, payment channels, dispute handling, security audits, or upstream service rules require a longer period.

1. Account information: retained while the account exists; after account cancellation, it will be deleted or anonymized within the scope permitted by law, but information necessary for accounting, disputes, security, and compliance may continue to be retained for the necessary period;
2. Order, top-up, refund, and accounting records: retained for no less than 6 months;
3. API call metadata: retained for no more than 90 days;
4. Error logs, troubleshooting logs, and security logs: retained for no more than 90 days; retention may be extended for security incidents, disputes, fraud, violations, or regulatory requirements;
5. Customer support, appeal, and proof materials actively submitted by users: retained for no more than 1 year after processing is completed, unless the law requires otherwise;
6. API input and output text: not retained long term by default; if users enable logs, cache, fine-tuning, file, knowledge base, or troubleshooting functions, retention follows the corresponding feature description.

## 6. Information Sharing, Entrusted Processing, and Third Parties

To provide the service, GGUUAI may provide, share, or entrust relevant information to the following third parties within the necessary scope:

1. Upstream model providers, to complete model calls, return outputs, and execute content safety or model capabilities;
2. Cloud platforms, network service providers, CDNs, storage, logging, monitoring, and security service providers, for hosting, transmission, storage, troubleshooting, and security protection;
3. Payment channels, banks, collection service providers, invoice or accounting service providers, for top-ups, refunds, reconciliation, invoices, and risk review;
4. Risk-control, anti-fraud, security, content safety, and compliance service providers, to identify abnormalities, attacks, fraud, abuse, and violations;
5. Customer support, ticketing, email, or communication tools, to handle inquiries, complaints, appeals, and notices;
6. Regulators, judicial authorities, law-enforcement agencies, arbitration institutions, upstream providers, or other legally authorized entities, when required by laws and regulations, regulatory requirements, judicial procedures, mandatory upstream requirements, or protection of lawful rights and interests.

We will require third parties to process relevant information only within the necessary scope and to take reasonable security measures. However, third-party services may have their own independent terms, privacy policies, and data processing rules. You should understand and comply with them before using relevant models, payment channels, or third-party capabilities.

## 7. Cross-border and Cross-region Processing

GGUUAI provides services to regions where it is permitted by law, supported by payment channels, allowed by upstream service policies, and approved by platform risk assessment. To provide model calls, cloud hosting, network transmission, payment settlement, risk control, security, customer support, or technical support, your information may be transferred, stored, or processed between legally permitted regions.

If relevant processing involves cross-border or cross-region transfer of personal information, GGUUAI will take necessary measures within the scope required by applicable law, including but not limited to informing users of the processing purpose, data types, recipient categories, retention period, security measures, and methods for exercising user rights.

If your or your end users' region has special requirements for data export, cross-border transfer, industry data, sensitive information, or personal information, you should confirm before use whether the service is suitable for your business scenario and, if necessary, sign an additional data processing agreement with GGUUAI.

## 8. User Rights

To the extent permitted and required by applicable law, you may contact [support@gguuai.com](mailto:support@gguuai.com) to request access to, correction of, copying of, or deletion of your personal information; withdraw authorization; cancel your account; restrict or object to specific processing; or obtain an explanation of personal information processing rules.

To protect account security, GGUUAI may require you to verify identity and provide account information, order information, or other necessary materials. For requests where identity cannot be verified, others' rights may be affected, security risk control is involved, accounting audit or dispute handling is involved, legal obligations or regulatory requirements apply, or technical implementation is not possible, GGUUAI may lawfully refuse or delay processing and explain the reason to you.

After account cancellation, you may no longer be able to access your account, API keys, balance, history, configurations, and related services. Cancellation does not affect orders, consumption, violations, disputes, indemnification, refund review, compliance audits, or legal responsibilities that occurred before cancellation.

## 9. Data Security

GGUUAI will take reasonable technical and management measures to protect information security, including access control, permission management, transmission security, log auditing, key protection, security monitoring, abnormality detection, and minimum necessary access.

You should also take necessary security measures, including properly safeguarding API keys, rotating keys regularly, avoiding public exposure of keys on the frontend, limiting key permissions, setting reasonable credit limits, enabling necessary access restrictions, monitoring abnormal consumption, and promptly deleting leaked keys.

If a data security incident occurs that may affect your rights and interests, GGUUAI will take remedial measures as required by applicable laws and regulations and notify affected users or competent authorities through reasonable methods.
