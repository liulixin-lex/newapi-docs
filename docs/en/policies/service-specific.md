# 5. Service-specific Terms

## 1. API Relay and Model Access

GGUUAI provides relay, aggregation, routing, and access services for artificial intelligence models, helping users access different models, channels, or related capabilities through a unified interface.

Some models, interface capabilities, model names, context lengths, response speeds, availability, regional access, content safety policies, error types, tool capabilities, image or file capabilities, cache rules, billing methods, and data processing methods may be determined by upstream model providers, cloud platforms, network routes, regulatory requirements, or platform risk-control policies.

GGUUAI may change, restrict, suspend, or remove available models, routing policies, multipliers, rate limits, context lengths, cache rules, channel capabilities, or service capabilities based on upstream service changes, cost changes, regulatory requirements, risk-control policies, system maintenance, model stability, or business adjustments.

You must confirm yourself that the selected model, interface, context length, capability scope, price, and usage restrictions are suitable for your business scenario, and you must comply with applicable rules of the relevant upstream model providers.

## 2. API Usage Limits

You must comply with API usage limits displayed, notified, or actually enforced by GGUUAI, including but not limited to:

1. Rate limits: you must not exceed the call frequency specified by the platform or enforced by the system;
2. Concurrency limits: you must not exceed the concurrency quota corresponding to your account, model, channel, project, or package;
3. Quota limits: you must not exceed allocated credits, package credits, model credits, or risk-control credits;
4. Context limits: you must not submit context, files, or parameters beyond the supported scope of a model or channel;
5. Cache rules: if the platform supports caching, you must comply with cache duration, scope, fees, and clearing rules;
6. Content safety limits: you must not submit or generate content that violates the Acceptable Use Policy;
7. Monitoring and auditing: GGUUAI may monitor API usage for security, billing, risk control, troubleshooting, compliance, and service optimization;
8. Anti-circumvention: you must not bypass restrictions by splitting requests, rotating accounts, rotating IPs, falsifying identities, falsifying end users, hiding the real purpose, or other methods.

## 3. Beta, Experimental, or Preview Capabilities

GGUUAI may from time to time provide beta, preview, experimental models, temporary interfaces, candidate features, gray-release capabilities, early-access capabilities, or other non-generally released capabilities.

Such capabilities may be unstable, incomplete, error-prone, inconsistent in response, changed at any time, downgraded, suspended, or removed.

Unless GGUUAI expressly states that they may be used in production, you should not use such capabilities in critical production scenarios, high-risk scenarios, strong-consistency scenarios, non-reversible scenarios, or scenarios that significantly affect life and health, property rights, or public interests.

You are responsible for business interruption, incorrect results, data loss, increased costs, customer complaints, third-party claims, or other losses arising from your use of or reliance on such capabilities.

## 4. Files, Cache, Fine-tuning, Knowledge Bases, and Model Customization

If GGUUAI provides file upload, caching, context storage, knowledge base, vectorization, batch processing, evaluation, fine-tuning, model customization, model deployment, or similar capabilities, you must ensure that submitted data, files, materials, and content have lawful sources, sufficient authorization, and comply with applicable laws and regulations.

Data used for fine-tuning, customization, evaluation, caching, knowledge bases, or similar capabilities remains user content. GGUUAI does not obtain ownership of your user content because you use those capabilities.

Related training, processing, storage, or caching is used only to provide the corresponding capability to you and is not considered GGUUAI's use of your API inputs and outputs to train general-purpose models, unless you separately and expressly opt in to a data authorization or development cooperation program.

You must back up original data, training materials, configurations, result files, evaluation records, model versions, and business data yourself. Unless otherwise expressly stated in writing, GGUUAI does not promise long-term retention of your files, data, configurations, caches, fine-tuning materials, result models, or historical versions.

## 5. Third-party Models, Cloud Platforms, and Upstream Services

Some GGUUAI services may depend on third-party model providers, cloud platforms, network routes, payment channels, content safety services, logging services, monitoring services, data processing services, or other third-party infrastructure.

Policy adjustments, regional restrictions, price changes, interface changes, authentication changes, content safety policy changes, data processing rule changes, failures, degradation, throttling, termination, or regulatory restrictions affecting third-party services may affect GGUUAI services.

GGUUAI will use reasonable efforts to handle related impacts, but does not promise that third-party services will always be available, error-free, delay-free, or meet your specific needs.

You must not use GGUUAI to bypass access restrictions, usage restrictions, regional restrictions, content safety restrictions, or technical restrictions set by third-party providers, payment channels, cloud platforms, regulators, sanctions rules, or applicable laws.

## 6. Service Levels and Technical Support

GGUUAI will use reasonable commercial efforts to maintain service stability and availability. However, unless GGUUAI signs an express SLA with you, any statement about availability, response time, recovery time, error rate, throughput, concurrency, or technical support hours does not constitute a binding commitment or basis for compensation.

If GGUUAI provides an SLA for a specific package, enterprise plan, dedicated channel, or cloud-hosted service, the corresponding order, contract, announcement, or written agreement will prevail.

Planned maintenance, emergency maintenance, system upgrades, failure repairs, migration, risk-control handling, upstream service changes, or force majeure may cause temporary service unavailability, delay, or capability changes. GGUUAI will provide advance notice or post-event explanation within a reasonable and feasible scope.
